NetHack History

Behold, mortal, the origins of NetHack...

Jay Fenlason wrote the original Hack, with help from Kenny Woodland,
Mike Thome, and Jon Payne.

Andries Brouwer did a major re-write while at Stichting Mathematisch Centrum,
transforming Hack into a very different game.  He released Hack 1.0 in
December 1984, with the final version 1.0.3 in July 1985.

Mike Stephenson merged these various versions back together, incorporating
many of the added features, and produced NetHack version 1.4 in 1987.  He
then coordinated a cast of thousands in enhancing and debugging NetHack 1.4
and released NetHack versions 2.2 and 2.3.

Later, Mike coordinated a major re-write of the game, heading the NetHack
Development Team to produce NetHack 3.0c.  Version 3.0 went through ten
patch-level revisions.

The 3.1 Development Team re-structured the game's design, and re-wrote major
parts of the code.  They added multiple dungeons, a new display, special
individual character quests, a new endgame and many other new features.
Version 3.1.0 was released in January 1993.

Version 3.2.0 was released in April 1996, marking the tenth anniversary of
the development team.  During the interval between 3.1.3 and 3.2.0, one of
the founding members of the NetHack Development Team, Dr. Izchak Miller,
passed away.  That release was dedicated to him.

Version 3.3.0 was released in December 1999.  It was the first version to
separate race and profession, adding dwarves, gnomes, and orcs alongside the
familiar human race.  Monk and Ranger roles were introduced.

Version 3.4.0 was released in March 2002.  The subsequent 3.4.3 release in
December 2003 proved remarkably stable, providing continued enjoyment by the
community for more than a decade.

Version 3.6.0 was released in December 2015, merging work done by the
development team since 3.4.3 with some beloved community patches.  Near the
end of its development, author Terry Pratchett passed away; the release
introduced a tribute to him.

Subsequent 3.6.x releases (through 3.6.7 in February 2023) contained
security fixes and bug fixes.

NetHack Babel is a Rust reimplementation of the NetHack engine, aiming
for behavioral alignment with the C codebase while providing a modern,
safe, and extensible foundation.

The official NetHack web site is https://www.nethack.org/.
